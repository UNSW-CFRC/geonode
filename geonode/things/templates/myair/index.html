Carbon dioxide (CO2)

What is CO2?

Each carbon dioxide (CO2) molecule has a carbon atom double bonded to two oxygen atoms.

CO2 is a colourless odorless gas occuring naturally in Earth's atmosphere at trace levels.

CO2 in your room

Indoor CO2 ranges from atmospheric 405 ppm to over 2500 ppm depending on room occupancy and ventilation.

Research indicates that elevated CO2 impacts decision-making above 1000 ppm and may have a significant impact at 2500 ppm.

CO2 in our atmosphere

CO2 has now reached over 405 ppm (parts per million) in our atmosphere, 35% higher than any time before the industrial revolution for at least the last 800,000 years. This is a "direct existential threat" according to the UN Secretary-General.
